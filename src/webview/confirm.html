Important information about the fixed portion of your loan

No access to redraw

You can't redraw from a fixed rate loan

Changes to your offset account

Your offset account won't offset the interest on any fixed portion of your loan

Fixed loan term end date

When your fixed loan term expires, your loan will switch back to a variable interest rate and you'll be left with two separate loan accounts. If you want to continue, you'll have 10 days before your term expires to lock

Repayments

When we've processed your change you'll need to arrange your repayment for the new fixed loan account


Fees and charges

Breaking the fixed portion of your loan

You may incur an economic cost if you choose to break the fixed period of your loan. Examples of breaking your loan early include:

  • Paying out and closing your loan
  • Making additional payments of more than $20,000 over the term of your fixed loan
  • Changing from fixed rate to variable rate early
  • Changing the purpose of your loan

Monthly service fee

You'll incur an $8.00 monthly loan service fee on the new fixed loan account. Refer to your home loan general terms and conditions for more information
